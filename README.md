## Research Question

To what extent are HR occupations exposed to Generative AI automation, and which specific task clusters and skills drive that exposure?

---

## Key Findings

- **HR Assistants** show the highest AI exposure score (3.95/5.0) — 58% of their O*NET task statements are classified as high-exposure
- **Education Administrators** show the lowest exposure score (3.00/5.0) — only 22% high-exposure task statements
- **Skills analysis reveals a bifurcation** — Active Listening and Social Perceptiveness are AI-resistant while Critical Thinking and Writing show higher AI exposure
- **38% of all HR task statements** analyzed fall in the high-exposure category
- Even senior roles such as **HR Managers score in the moderate-exposure range** (3.31/5.0) — no HR occupation is untouched

---

## Important Note on Methodology

Exposure scores reflect the proportion of O*NET documented task statements classified at each exposure level. They do not represent the proportion of actual working time devoted to those tasks, nor do they indicate the likelihood of role elimination.

## Methodology

1. Downloaded O*NET 30.2 task and skills data for 8 HR occupations
2. Filtered 18,796 tasks to 180 HR-specific task statements across 8 SOC codes
3. Scored each task statement 1-5 for Gen AI exposure using keyword-based rubric grounded in McKinsey and WEF automation frameworks
4. Calculated average exposure score per occupation
5. Cross-referenced with O*NET skills importance ratings to identify AI-resistant and higher-exposure competencies
